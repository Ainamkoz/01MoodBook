Fresh Lemon, Pea and Spinach Soup

Ingredients
For the soup
  • 5 teaspoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Coarse salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 1 3/4 cups homemade or low-sodium store-bought chicken stock
For the sauce
  • 8 ounces spinach (curly or flat leaf), tough stems discarded (about 6 cups packed leaves)
  • 1/2 teaspoons fresh lemon juicee
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • 3/4 cup tiny greens, such as micro greens or mache
  • sliced green onions and nori, to finish
  • 1 pound fresh English peas, shelled (about 1 cup)r
  • Coarse salt and freshly ground pepper
Nutrition Facts
Serves 4
  • Total Fat : 20.4g

Description

Lemon is nature’s seasoning and I use it whenever I can, so making it one of the principal flavours in this soup lightens up the pea and spinach flavours, and allows the soup to be served either hot or cold. In the evening I have been enjoying the soup hot in a small bowl at the start my dinner before I dive into my plate of veggies, protein, and salad … and it is doing the trick by filling me up.

I have also tried the soup cold for lunch, and as a mid afternoon snack when the “munchies” hit me, by sipping it from a small glass as a form of “veggie smoothie” and it is delicious … not too thick, velvety smooth, and quite filling, all of which helps to keep the hunger pains at bay. Just add a little seasoning to it before sipping. You don’t need to include the “topping”, but as part of my experimenting I added it as I do like to “crunch” on something, so if you are like me then this is something you may like too … I must admit it does add a nice touch, particularly if you are entertaining.

Instructions

  • 01. Heat 4 teaspoons oil in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat. Add onion, garlic, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and a pinch of pepper. Cook, stirring, 3 minutes. Add stock, and bring to a boil.
  • 02. Add peas, and return to a boil. Reduce heat, and simmer, stirring occasionally, until peas are tender and bright green, 2 to 3 minutes. Stir in spinach. Cover, and cook, stirring occasionally, until spinach has wilted, 2 to 3 minutes.
  • 03. Working in 2 batches, puree pea mixture in a blender, filling no more than halfway and adding up to 2 tablespoons water to each batch to achieve desired consistency. Stir in 1 teaspoon lemon juice, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and 1/8 teaspoon pepper.
  • 04. Divide the soup among 4 bowls. Toss greens with remaining teaspoon oil and 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice; season with salt and pepper. Divide salad among frico bowls. Float a frico bowl on top of each soup. Serve immediately.
